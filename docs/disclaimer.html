Yields shown are protocol-reported snapshots that change in real time. Past performance does not predict future results.

Tokenized assets carry issuer risk

  • Backed xStocks (NVDAx, AAPLx, TSLAx, SPYx): 1:1 backed tokens issued by Backed Assets (JE) Limited under an EU prospectus. They are securities, not stablecoins. They are not available to U.S. persons. Read the issuer's documentation before holding.
  • Ondo USDY: A tokenized US Treasury exposure issued by Ondo USDY LLC. It is a security, not a stablecoin. Each share accrues yield and trades at a price that grows over time (so 1 USDC ≠ 1 USDY). Not available to U.S. persons. Read the issuer's documentation.

Smart contract and protocol risk

Transactions constructed by autoyield interact with third-party Solana programs (Jupiter, Raydium, Orca, Meteora, the issuer's own programs). These programs may have undiscovered bugs, may be upgraded in ways that change behavior, or may be exploited.

Network and execution risk

  • Solana network congestion can cause transactions to fail (we set conservative priority fees but cannot guarantee inclusion).
  • RPC endpoints can return stale or incorrect data.
  • Liquidity in tokenized-asset pools is often shallow; small trades can move price meaningfully.
  • MEV bots can extract value from your trade.

Slippage and price risk

autoyield enforces a default 0.5% slippage cap (hard ceiling 1%). Even within these bounds, the price you receive can be worse than the displayed quote, especially in volatile markets.

Tax

Crypto transactions, including swaps between USDC and tokenized securities, may be taxable events in your jurisdiction. autoyield does not generate tax forms. You are responsible for your own records.

Limit your exposure

Only use amounts you can afford to lose entirely. Test with the smallest possible amount before scaling up. Keep your seed phrase offline. Revoke unused token approvals.
